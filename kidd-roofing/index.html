The seam: leads that misroute between offices and quietly die

When one HubSpot portal serves six offices in three states, a lead is captured centrally and then has to be routed to the right office and the right rep. Unless that location-aware routing is explicitly architected (it rarely is in mid-market roofing), a fraction of leads land on the wrong office, a round-robin slot, or an owner who has moved on. Marketing shows the lead came in. The office that should have called never knew it existed.

This is the textbook definition of a seam: the exact point where two systems disagree about the truth. The marketing system says "lead delivered." The sales floor says "what lead?" No alarm fires, because no single dashboard sees both sides. So it is not treated as an emergency, even while it bleeds. A visible leak gets fixed same day. A silent one runs for years.

Check it yourself in two minutes

  1. In HubSpot, filter contacts created in the last 90 days where lead owner is blank, or owner is someone no longer in that role.
  2. Pull contacts whose office / location property is empty but who submitted a location-specific form.
  3. Cross-check a week of web leads against what each office says it actually received.

If any of those lists is not empty, the seam is live, and the number above is no longer hypothetical.
